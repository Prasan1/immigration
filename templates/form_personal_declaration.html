Personal Statement / Declaration

In Support of Immigration Application

How to Use This Template:
  • Fill in fields: Enter your personal information in the underlined fields
  • Edit sections: Click the gray boxes to write your story - each section has examples to guide you
  • Use examples: The blue example boxes show you what to write - customize them for your situation
  • Follow tips: Green tip boxes highlight important points to remember
  • Print/Save: When done, click "Print" or "Save as PDF" to export your completed declaration
  • Review: Have your attorney review before submitting - this is a legal document submitted under penalty of perjury
Quick Tips for a Strong Declaration:
  1. Be specific: Use exact dates, names, places, and amounts
  2. Be honest: Lying can result in denial and a permanent bar from the U.S.
  3. Be detailed: More detail is better - immigration officers appreciate thoroughness
  4. Be organized: Follow the sections provided and stay on topic
  5. Be professional: Use proper grammar and avoid slang
  6. Get help: Consult an immigration attorney to review your declaration

I am submitting this personal statement in support of my application. The following facts are true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.
Sample Opening: "I am a [nationality] citizen currently residing in the United States. I entered the U.S. on [date] on a [visa type]. I am applying for [benefit] through my [relationship], [petitioner's name], who is a U.S. citizen/permanent resident..."

Background and Personal History:

What to include: Your nationality, birthplace, family background, education, and how you came to the U.S.
Example: "I am a citizen of [country], born on [date] in [city]. I grew up in a loving family with my parents and [number] siblings. I completed my education at [school name], earning a degree in [field]. In [year], I decided to come to the United States to [reason - pursue education/join family/seek better opportunities]..."
Tip: Be specific about dates, places, and names. Immigration officers appreciate detail and honesty.

Immigration History:

What to include: All entries to the U.S., visa types, status changes, work authorization, and any immigration issues.
Example: "I first entered the United States on [date] at [port of entry] on a [visa type]. I was admitted until [date]. Since then, I have [maintained my status/changed status to/extended my stay]. I have [never violated/once violated] my immigration status. [If applicable: On [date], I overstayed my visa because...]"
Important: Be completely honest about any violations, overstays, or denials. Your attorney can help address these issues, but hiding them can result in denial or even a ban.

Relationship / Family Ties (if applicable):

What to include: How you met your spouse/petitioner, when you married, your daily life together, evidence of genuine relationship.
Example: "I met my spouse, [name], in [year] through [how you met]. We began dating on [date] and married on [date] in [location]. We live together at [address] and share all household expenses. We have [number] children together, [names and ages]. Our relationship is genuine and based on mutual love and respect..."
Tip: Include specific details about your daily routine, shared activities, joint finances, and future plans. USCIS looks for evidence of a genuine relationship.

Employment and Financial Situation:

What to include: Current job, work history, income, how you support yourself and your family.
Example: "I am currently employed as a [job title] at [company name] in [city], where I have worked since [date]. My annual salary is approximately $[amount]. My duties include [brief description]. Prior to this, I worked as [previous jobs]. I am able to financially support myself and my family without public assistance..."
Tip: Show that you are financially stable and not likely to become a public charge. Include all sources of income and assets.

Community Ties and Good Moral Character:

What to include: Community involvement, volunteer work, religious activities, friends, neighbors, clean criminal record.
Example: "I am an active member of my community. I volunteer at [organization] every [frequency] where I [activities]. I attend [church/temple/mosque] regularly and participate in [activities]. I have developed close friendships with neighbors and coworkers. I have never been arrested or convicted of any crime. I pay my taxes on time and follow all laws..."
Tip: Letters of recommendation from community members, employers, or religious leaders can support your statement.

Hardship / Reason for Request (if applicable):

What to include: Specific hardships you or your U.S. citizen/LPR family members would suffer if denied. Focus on extreme hardship.
Example: "If my application is denied and I am required to return to [country], my U.S. citizen spouse would suffer extreme hardship. [He/She] has lived in the U.S. for [years] and has no ties to [country]. [He/She] does not speak [language]. Our children, who are U.S. citizens, would be forced to choose between their parents or face separation from [me/their mother/father]. Additionally, [describe medical, financial, educational, or other specific hardships]..."
Tip: "Extreme hardship" is a legal standard. Consult your attorney about what qualifies. Focus on hardship to your U.S. citizen/LPR family member, not just to yourself.

Conclusion:

What to include: Summary of why you're requesting this benefit and what it means to you and your family.
Example: "I respectfully request that USCIS approve my application for [benefit]. This approval would allow me to continue building my life in the United States with my family, contributing to my community, and pursuing my dreams. I am committed to being a law-abiding, productive member of American society. I promise to continue to work hard, pay taxes, and give back to the country that has given me so much opportunity. Thank you for considering my application."
Tip: Be sincere and heartfelt. This is your opportunity to make a personal connection with the officer reviewing your case.
